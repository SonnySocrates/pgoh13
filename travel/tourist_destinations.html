Popular destinations for your holidays  

There is nothing worse than coming back from a trip abroad to hear friends ask "Have you been to such-and-such a place?" and having to reply in the negative.
This page puts the focus on other must-see cities which are just a couple of hours from where you are holidaying. Thus if you are in Paris for two or three weeks in summer and you love the seaside it would be a real pity not to take the train for La Rochelle. And once you are in La Rochelle, how about going to the bicycle-riding paradise of Ile de Ré? After all it is only a bridge away!



Again, taking Paris as our base (since hardly any tourist comes to Europe without making a stop in this enchanting city) you will hardly lose any of your valuable holiday time if you were to take the night train for either Madrid, Barcelona, Venice or Rome.
Think about it - you spend the night sleeping as you normally do (though the sleeping berth can never be as comfortable as your bed) and when you wake up in the morning you are already in Madrid, Barcelona, Venice or Rome! Once you are in Rome it's only one and a half hour's train ride to Naples. And once you are in Naples you will never forgive yourself if you don't take the boat to the Capri Island...The possibilities are endless. They will be discussed more fully in these pages. (Click on the name of the city you are interested in.) Experienced travellers will tell you that the weeks that you spend planning your coming holiday trip could sometimes be even more exciting than the trip itself. But as with everything else in life you have to make a choice.
Have no doubt about it, you just can't see or do everything that you would like to during your short stay. It's even worse if you are just at the doorstep of another city that is just as exciting and you missed it out simply because you did not have the necessary information. That would be a shame because Or if you are a jet-setter who happens to be in Paris, how about hopping over to the French Riviera which to most people conjures up magic names like Cannes, Nice and Monaco. Ok, sorry we're not in Rome yet. And you are not making a world trip, I know!
But as planning your holidays is such an exciting game, I'll continue. Once in Madrid, and after having seen all of it (or the must-see places anyway), a train ride of just half an hour will take you to the historic town of Toledo or if you are in London in summer, you might consider taking the hour-long train journey to the seaside resort of Brighton. The cities here are in Europe, China, Morocco and Malaysia. Suggestions are always given on how you can "kill two birds with one stone" (and why not three?) by hopping to a neighbouring city really worth exploring from where you happen to be. You are guided on finding your way around in each city - especially upon your arrival at the railway station or airport. I have a soft spot particularly for travellers on a low budget so wherever possible there are suggestions on how to get by with the minimum amount of spending. Remember that if you are prepared to hunt around for tips and read visitors' accounts in travel forums you will often be able to find really cheap places to stay or travel around cheaply or eat in economic restaurants even in expensive cities like London or Venice. */?>
