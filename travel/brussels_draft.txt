Bruges is one of the more well-known World Heritage sites of UNESCO.  Together with Amsterdam it is often referred to as the "Venice of the North". (pic of canal) Much of its medieval architecture is still visible today.  The brick spire of its Church of Our Lady building (…glise Notre-Dame), dating as far back as the 13th century, reaches 122.3m making it the second tallest brickwork tower in the world.
Another 13th century monument representative of Bruges is its belfry.
