The script is wrong because it always subtracts 1 year, regardless of whether the person's birthday has already occurred this year. The problematic line: JavaScriptage=annee_dujour-annee_naissance-1 This logic assumes the birthday has not yet passed in the current year. It is a very crude (and incorrect) way to calculate age. Example with your date: Birth: 21 January 1988 Today: May 2026 (as of the current date) Correct age = 38 (birthday already passed in January). What the script does: Current year = 2026 Birth year = 1988 2026 - 1988 - 1 = 37 It gives 37 because it blindly subtracts 1. When would it be correct? Only if today is before January 21st. For example, on December 31st 2025, the person would indeed be 37, and the script would correctly output 37. Better way to calculate age You need to compare month and day as well, not just the year.
